Repair and Alteration Option

There are a number of options to repair the Woolen Mill dam and to alter it to increase its capacity

Repairs Many of the repairs needed for the Woolen Mill dam are straightforward, seepage, and damaged masonry and concrete can all be repaired with common construction solutions.
Capacity alterations The most challenging aspect of the Woolen Mill dam is its limited capacity. Its total capacity is ~3,590 - ~4,300 cfs. As required by the Wisconsin DNR the Woolen Mill dam would need to pass a 500 year flood, a flow of ~6,940 cfs, which requires increasing the dams spillway capacity by 2,640 - 2,990 cfs an increase of ~75%. There are a few options to increase the capacity of the Woolen Mill dam, a spillway gate can be installed in the dam, the height of the dam can be reduced, the dam can be lengthened, the spillway face can be re-profiled, or a combination of these can be used. All of these solutions are extensive, disruptive, and expensive projects. A combination of these strategies would likely need to be used to increase the Woolen Mill dams capacity because of the tight space of the site. Any of, or a combination of these strategies would significantly alter the dam, and threaten the original limestone wing wall if the dam is lengthened.
