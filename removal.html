Full Removal Option

In order to address the Woolen Mill dam's capacity and condition issues the simplest solution is to remove the existing dam and complete any necessary masonry repair on the limestone wingwall. Removal of the Woolen Mill dam is preferable to repair and alteration for a number of reasons.

Cost Removing the Woolen Mill dam will be far less expensive than replacing or repairing and altering the current one. This cost saving was demonstrated by the removal of the West Bend Woolen Mill dam in 1988, the cost to replace the dam was $3.3 million, compared to $86,000 to remove the dam and $200,000 to build a park. In addition removal also eliminates all future costs of owning and operating the dam, including inspections, maintenance, and insurance costs. The Woolen Mill dam is currently mostly owned by the City of Cedarburg, removing the dam eliminates all future costs for the City of Cedarburg. This translates into saved tax dollars at the local level. It also eliminates costs of the Wisconsin DNR, that translates to saved tax dollars at the state level.
Water Quality Improvement Dams have detrimental effects on water quality by creating an impoundment behind them. This changes the river geometry resulting in slower water movement through the impoundment. This causes accumulation of organic matter, and the river water to warm in the impoundment. This results in warm low oxygen water, unfavorable for many species. It also allows the river to drop its sediment load, this alters the streambed which impacts habitat for benthic organisms, as well as fills the impoundment with sediment further changing the river geometry and exacerbating water quality issues. Removing the Woolen Mill dam will improve the water quality in Cedar Creek in the area of the dams former impoundment.
Habitat Improvement Dams fragment habitats by presenting a barrier that blocks or restricts movement of aquatic organisms. As mentioned above they also alter streambeds and degrade water quality which impact aquatic organisms. Removing the Woolen Mill dam will restore a mile of Cedar Creek, increasing the amount of quality riverine habitat.
Historical Preservation The Woolen Mill dam is not historic because of its recent construction and limited industrial history(see Dam History, and Historic Issues), however the limestone wing wall arguably is since it is original to the mill. Its presence is threatened by alteration and repair of the dam. By removing the dam, the original limestone wing wall's preservation will be ensured.
Increased Safety The Woolen Mill dam raises the floodplain, creating a greater flood risk for surrounding properties. In addition, dams create dangerous currents that present a drowning risk. Removing the Woolen Mill dam will solve these issues.
Local examples Several dams have been removed successfully in Ozaukee County including the Lime Kiln and Chair Factory dams (Grafton), Concordia Mill dam (washout) (Cedarburg), Wisconsin Lutheran Seminary dam (Mequon), Newburg dam (Newburg), and Waubeka dam (Waubeka).
