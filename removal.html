Removal of a dam is often time preferable to repair for a number of reasons

Cost Removing a dam costs a fraction of what it costs to repair one. It also eliminates all future costs of owning and operating the dam, including inspections, maintenance, and insurance costs. The Woolen Mill dam is majority owned by the City of Cedarburg, removing the dam would save taxpayer dollars
Water quality Removing a dam improves water quality, clarity, dissolved oxygen, water temperature, and nutrient loads all improve with dam removal
Habitat improvement Dams fragment habitats by preventing or restricting movement of aquatic organisms above or below the dam. They also alter streambeds and degrade water quality which impact aquatic organisms.
Increased safety Dams can fail and cause flooding, small dams like the Woolen Mill, also present a drowning hazard because of dangerous currents near the base of the dam.
