Full Removal Option

In order to address the Woolen Mill dam's capacity and condition issues the simplest solution is to remove the existing dam and repair the masonry work. Removal of the Woolen Mill dam may be preferable to repair and alteration for a number of reasons.

Cost Removing the Woolen Mill dam will be far less expensive than the repairs and alterations needed. It also eliminates all future costs of owning and operating the dam, including inspections, maintenance, and insurance costs. Since the Woolen Mill dam is majority owned by the City of Cedarburg, removing the dam would mean saving taxpayer dollars.
Water Quality Improvement Dams have detrimental effects on water quality, removing the Woolen Mill dam will improve the water quality in Cedar Creek in the area of the dams former impoundment.
Habitat Improvement Dams fragment habitats by presenting a barrier that blocks or restricts movement of aquatic organisms. They also alter streambeds and degrade water quality which impact aquatic organisms.
Historical Preservation The Woolen Mill dam is not historic (see Dam History, and Historic Issues), by removing the dam, the original limestone wing wall's preservation will be ensured.
Floodplain Reduction Removing the Woolen Mill dam will lower the elevation of Cedar Creek, reducing the size of the current floodplain.
Increased Safety Dams are safety hazards, they create dangerous currents, removing the Woolen Mill dam will eliminate this hazard.
