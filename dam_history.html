History of the Woolen Mill dam

c1860

The construction of original Woolen Mill dam is completed (exact date unknown). The dam is a simple rock filled crib design.

The original Woolen Mill dam c1909, photo from the Rappold Collection

1881

The original dam washes out in a flood, the flooding is so severe that every mill in Cedarburg lost its dam except the Cedarburg Mill.

1896

The Woolen Mill is expanded to meet increasing demand, water power is deemed insufficient and steam power is installed. A boiler house, engine house, and large smokestack are constructed, both of which still exist today.

The engine house (left), boiler house (center), and smokestack 2020

1933

The dam washes out for the second time.

1938

After 5 years, construction begins on a new dam in front of the old one. The new dam is to be a modern concrete gravity dam with an efficient ogee crest, a far more advanced design compared to the original rock filled crib dam.

Woolen Mill Impoundment August 1937 just before construction of the new dam, note the small impoundment behind the remains of the original dam

1939

Construction on the new dam is completed. The remains of the original dam are removed during the construction of the new dam. The first inspection of the new dam takes place that August

New dam under construction c1939, photo from the Rappold Collection

1945

Weaving at the mill ceases, the mill continues making specialty items.

1946, 1957, and 1961

Dam is inspected

1968

After years of sitting idle the Woolen Mill shuts down just 29 years after the dam was rebuilt

1980, and 1986

Dam is inspected

2004, and 2005

Dam is inspected by Mead & Hunt, concrete and masonry deterioration on walls found, undermining of limestone wall reported. Calculations of the spillway reveal a total spillway capacity of 3,590 cfs, insufficient for the 100 year flood flow as required by the Wisconsin DNR at the time.

2008, 2011, and 2014

Dam is inspected

2018

Dam is inspected by GRAEF, concrete and masonry deterioration on walls found, undermining of limestone wall and possibly toe reported

2021

Work on the dam set to begin, which could be either alteration and repair, or removal
