History

1865

Construction of the Woolen Mill is completed, along with the original dam.

Woolen Mill Dam c1909

1896

The Woolen Mill is expanded to meet increasing demand, water power is deemed insufficient and steam power is installed. A boiler house and large smokestack are constructed both of which still exist today.

1933

The original dam washes out.

1938

After 5 years, construction begins on a new dam in front of the ruins of the old one. The new dam is a concrete gravity dam, a very modern and common design.

Woolen Mill Impoundment c1937 just before construction of the new dam, note the small impounment behind the remains of the original dam

1939

Construction on the new dam is completed.

New dam under construction c1939

1968

After years of sitting idle the Woolen Mill shuts down just 29 years after the dam was rebuilt

2007

Inspections of the dam reveal concerning conditions including, undermining at the toe, leakage around the abutments, and an inadequatly sized spillway

2018

2021

Work on the dam set to begin, which could be either alteration and repair, or removal
